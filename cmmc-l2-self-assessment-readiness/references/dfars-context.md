# DFARS and Regulatory Context

This document provides brief regulatory context for why customers request CMMC Level 2 self-assessments.

## 32 CFR Part 170 - CMMC Program

Part 170 establishes the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC) program. Key points:

- Requires contractors in the Defense Industrial Base (DIB) to achieve specific cybersecurity levels
- CMMC Level 2 aligns with NIST SP 800-171 requirements
- Self-assessments are permitted for certain contract types prior to formal C3PAO certification
- Annual self-assessments must be recorded in the Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS)

## DFARS 252.204-7012 - Safeguarding CUI and Cyber Incident Reporting

This clause requires contractors to:

- Implement NIST SP 800-171 security requirements
- Report cyber incidents to DoD within 72 hours
- Conduct malware analysis and provide results to DoD
- Preserve and protect images of affected systems

Applies to contractors that have Federal Contract Information (FCI) or Covered Defense Information (CDI).

## DFARS 252.204-7019 - Notice of NIST SP 800-171 DoD Assessment Requirements

This clause informs contractors that DoD may:

- Conduct assessments to verify implementation of NIST SP 800-171
- Use the DoD Assessment Methodology to evaluate compliance
- Require Medium or High assessments through DIBCAC
- Consider assessment scores in source selection

## DFARS 252.204-7020 - NIST SP 800-171 DoD Assessment Requirements

This clause requires contractors to:

- Have a current assessment (no more than 3 years old) of NIST SP 800-171 implementation
- Post assessment scores in SPRS
- Conduct assessments using the DoD Assessment Methodology
- Allow DoD to conduct or mandate assessments

## Why Customers Request Self-Assessments

Prime contractors and government customers request self-assessments for:

1. **Pre-award evaluation**: Assessing vendor cybersecurity posture before contract award
2. **Flowdown compliance**: Ensuring subcontractors meet DFARS requirements flowed down in contracts
3. **Risk management**: Understanding supply chain cybersecurity risks
4. **SPRS verification**: Validating that posted scores are accurate and defensible
5. **Gap closure tracking**: Monitoring progress on remediation plans for subcontractors

## Self-Assessment vs. C3PAO Certification

**Self-Assessment (Basic/Medium)**:
- Conducted by contractor personnel
- Uses DoD Assessment Methodology
- Requires attestation by responsible official
- Posted to SPRS annually
- Sufficient for many contracts

**C3PAO Certification (Medium/High)**:
- Conducted by certified third-party assessor
- Required for contracts explicitly demanding CMMC Level 2 certification
- More rigorous evidence requirements
- Valid for 3 years
- Emerging requirement as CMMC program matures

For most vendor self-assessment requests from customers, the deliverable is a Basic or Medium self-assessment using the DoD Assessment Methodology, not a full C3PAO certification.
